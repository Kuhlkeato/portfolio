Machine Learning

From my academia I had the opportunity to take a machine learning class. We got learn about regression, model building and the theory that goes into it. To demonstrate our learning we where asked to build a supervised machine learning algorithm to classify and identify Lego pieces. To tackle this problem I first knew data cleaning would be required and formatting. I loaded all of the training model images into an array and then proceeded to change them all to grayscale as it is easier to work with pixel colors of 0-255 rather than RGB. I then changed the image structure to be presented as its numerical value. This presented the numbers as an array and I associated each array entry to its classified label. By performing a logistical regression with a multi-class perception rather the one vs all, I was able to achieve a recognition rate of 94% from the testing model. This accuracy was sufficient for the course but could be altered by introducing a soft max cost function to refine the boundaries.

Data Analytics

I had the opportunity to explore a very large data set of my choosing and to refine/clean the data. Naturally being a hockey fan I choose to use collected NHL data from the last 10 years and perform an exploratory data analysis (EDA). From this I was able to look at various collected stats and form a couple of interesting conclusions on average shift length, best coach, best team... etc. To present my findings I created a Tableau Dashboard as it is more visually appearing for the average viewer rather than looking at code and a result. From this project I grew an appreciation for advanced stats in sports as I got to see stats from teams at a lower level rather than just if they won or lost.

Capstone

For my last full year of academia I had the pleasure and opportunity to lead a group of colleagues in the development of Free-Space Optical Communication system. We underwent research and development to fully meet deliverables set out by our supervising professor while achieving a fully functional prototype. The idea behind our communication system was to implement the idea of fibre optic without the fibre optic line. This was done by using a laser diode with TTL modulation transmitting a signal to a receiving photodiode. We worked with a 980nm Laser diode as safety was at the upmost importance and at 980nm we could keep the Max Permissible Exposure low with minimizing the affects to our overall goal.

Cadence Design

For my cadence design project I was tasked with building and optimizing a 6-bit full adder from the transistor level. To tackle this I spent a lot of time researching papers and development of a traditional full adder and looked for areas where transistors could be reduced. I quickly was able to identify that by using a SERF adder I could lower the traditional gates used from 28 down to 12. With this drastic reduction the speed of the full adder was able to be optimized to 307.5ps from 15ns. I then produced the associated layout design for the full-adder and stayed with in 130nm technology parameters.
